## The Reunion

<img src="assets/lonn-yoda.png" alt="Master Lonn" width="300" height="300" align="right" style="margin-left: 20px; margin-bottom: 20px;">

Master Lonn returned to Rudskogen after six months away. The doubt hit the moment the downhill came rushing at him‚Äîfaster than he remembered.

July 2025: 1:28.969. December morning: 1:30.290. Over a second slower. His hands felt almost right, the corners almost where they should be. Almost. Except Turn 2.

That downhill braking zone fought him. Lap after lap, the same question: brake here or trust it? His body knew the answer six months ago. Now it had to relearn.

_"Felt familiar,"_ Master Lonn said, _"but still looking for the correct turn-in and braking in T2."_

He was right. Turn 2 was bleeding 73% of the time loss. One corner. Nearly a full second per lap.

### The Click

Two days later, something shifted. The 50 sign marker flashed past and suddenly his foot knew when to hit the brakes. Not a guess. A memory returning. Trail through the corner, third gear at the apex, second before the hill.

The timer blinked: 1:29.691.

First sub-1:30 of the week. The rust was gone.

### The Hunt

December 21st, morning session. He went hunting for the limit.

Brake bias up to 56.5%. Track limits tested‚Äîfound nine of them the hard way. But somewhere in that exploration, his body stumbled onto something his mind hadn't planned.

The downhill that used to scare him? It didn't anymore. Light brake on entry, trail through the whole corner. And the throttle‚Äînot at the apex, not after it. _Before_ it. As soon as the car turned in, foot already squeezing the accelerator.

The timer stopped.

**1:28.572**

Not 1:28.9-something. Not close to July's ghost. _Faster._ Four tenths faster.

He'd beaten six months of rust, beaten his old self, beaten the doubt, all in one lap that was only a tenth and change from theoretical perfection.

The data told the story: 26% more acceleration force from that early throttle. Using 14% more of the tires' lateral grip. Braking harder but for less time.

Three days. 1.7 seconds found.

That afternoon, trying to dial it back 5% for consistency, everything fell apart. Snap oversteer. Frustration.
