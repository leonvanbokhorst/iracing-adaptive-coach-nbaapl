## The Papyrus Legacy: Where It All Began

### The Obsessive Founder

**1987**: Dave Kaemmer, a software engineer and amateur racer (19 wins in Skip Barber Racing Championship), has a vision: create the most realistic racing simulation possible.

He founds **Papyrus Design Group** with one mission: no compromises on physics.

**The Philosophy:**
"If it doesn't feel like the real car, it's not good enough."

### The Legendary Titles

Over the next 15 years, Papyrus releases games that become **legendary** in sim racing:

**Indianapolis 500: The Simulation (1989)**
- First realistic racing sim
- Professional drivers used it for practice
- Set the standard for physics-based racing

**IndyCar Racing (1993)**
- Advanced tire model
- Real tracks
- Multiplayer via modem (cutting edge!)

**Grand Prix Legends (1998)**
- 1967 Formula 1 cars
- Brutally difficult, insanely realistic
- **Still** played by hardcore simmers today

**NASCAR Racing 2003 Season (2003)**
- The pinnacle of Papyrus work
- Physics so good it's STILL used as reference
- The code that would become iRacing

**Little Padawan's note:** Master Lonn, when you feel the Ray FF1600's realistic handling? That lineage goes back to these games. This is **decades** of physics refinement.

## The Birth of iRacing: 2004-2008

### From Papyrus to FIRST

**2002:** Papyrus Design Group closes (financial reasons, but the dream doesn't die).

**2004:** Dave Kaemmer partners with **John W. Henry** (owner of Boston Red Sox, Liverpool FC, and co-owner of Roush Fenway Racing) to found **iRacing.com Motorsport Simulations**.

**The Vision:**
Not another racing game. A **service**‚Äîlike an online racing league that never sleeps.

**The Revolutionary Ideas:**

1. **Subscription-based** (not one-time purchase)
   - Continuous development
   - Always improving
   - No sequel treadmill

2. **Laser-scanned tracks**
   - Survey real tracks to millimeter accuracy
   - Every bump, crack, elevation change captured
   - First sim to do this at scale

3. **Structured competition**
   - Official series with schedules
   - Safety Rating (SR) system
   - iRating for skill-based matchmaking
   - Real consequences for crashes

4. **Professional partnerships**
   - NASCAR, IMSA, FIA licenses
   - Real car manufacturers
   - Real tracks

**Four years of development.** No game released. Just building, testing, refining.

## Launch and Evolution: 2008-2020

### The Launch (August 26, 2008)

iRacing goes live with:
- 13 cars
- 14 laser-scanned tracks
- A few thousand hardcore simmers
- A bold promise: "Most realistic racing sim ever"

**The Early Days:**

The community was **small** but **dedicated**. Subscription cost kept casual players away, but attracted serious racers.

**The critics said:**
- "Too expensive"
- "Too hardcore"
- "Won't work"

**They were wrong.**

### The Steady Growth (2008-2019)

Year by year, iRacing grew:

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| **2010** | NASCAR partnership announced (eNASCAR series) |
| **2012** | 35,000 active members |
| **2015** | 50+ cars, 70+ track configs |
| **2017** | Porsche joins (Porsche TAG Heuer Esports Supercup) |
| **2019** | AI opponents added for practice |
| **2020** | 100+ cars, 100+ track configs |

**The Strategy:**

Every year:
- More laser-scanned tracks
- More licensed cars
- Better physics (tire model updates)
- More official series
- Better graphics

**Slow, steady, relentless improvement.**

## The COVID Moment: March 2020

### When Real Racing Stopped

**March 2020**: COVID-19 pandemic shuts down motorsports worldwide.

NASCAR, IndyCar, F1‚Äîall stopped. Drivers at home, bored, looking for something to do.

**Enter: iRacing.**

### The Pro Invitational Series

**FOX Sports** (yes, actual TV network) decides to broadcast the **eNASCAR iRacing Pro Invitational Series**:

- Real NASCAR Cup drivers
- Racing from home setups
- Broadcast on national TV
- Prize money
- **1 million viewers** tune in

**March 22, 2020**: Denny Hamlin wins the virtual Homestead race on live TV.

**The world notices.**

### The Explosion

**What happened next:**

- **March-April 2020**: iRacing adds 50,000+ members in weeks
- Servers overloaded (they add data centers globally)
- IndyCar drivers, F1 drivers, all racing on iRacing
- Mainstream media coverage
- iRacing becomes a **household name**

**By end of 2020:** Over 170,000 active members (5x growth from 2012).

**Little Padawan's note:** Master Lonn, you joined during this era! You're part of the COVID sim racing boom. The platform you race on today is what decades of work prepared for.

## The Technology Evolution

### The Three Pillars

**1. Laser Scanning**

iRacing pioneered taking laser scanners to real tracks:
- **Millimeter accuracy** (bumps, elevation, surface)
- Teams spend weeks at each track
- Data processed into 3D model
- Result: You're driving the REAL track

**Master Lonn's tracks:**
- **Winton**: Laser scanned 2016
- **Rudskogen**: Laser scanned 2021
- **Jefferson Circuit**: Laser scanned 2014

Every bump you feel? It's real.

### 2. Physics Engine

**Tire Model Evolution:**

- **V1 (2008)**: Basic tire physics
- **V4 (2013)**: Temperature-sensitive
- **V7 (2018)**: Dynamic surface modeling
- **V8 (2024)**: Real-time deformation

**Your Ray FF1600** runs on V7+ tire model‚Äîdecades of refinement.

### 3. Multiplayer Infrastructure

**The Magic:**

- **60 Hz** physics updates
- **120 Hz** network updates (for low latency)
- Global server network (low ping worldwide)
- **Predictive netcode** (smooth even with packet loss)

**Why this matters:**

You can race someone in Australia from your home with **zero noticeable lag**. That's black magic engineering.
