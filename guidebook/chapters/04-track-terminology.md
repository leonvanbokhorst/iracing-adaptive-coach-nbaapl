# Track Terminology Guide - Corner Types & Racing Lines

**A Practical Guide for the Ray FF1600 Driver**

Understanding what you're driving through helps you know HOW to drive it. Here's your reference for corner types, combinations, and track features.

---

## üîÑ Corner Types (By Radius & Speed)

### 1. **Hairpin**

**What it is:**
- Very tight, low-speed corner (usually 180¬∞ or close to it)
- Sharp radius, requires maximum rotation
- Usually 2nd gear in the Ray FF1600
- Entry: ~25-40 mph

**Characteristics:**
- Shortest radius of any corner type
- Hard braking required
- Requires quick rotation on entry
- Exit speed is KING (long straight usually follows)

**Technique:**
- Brake hard in straight line
- Long trail brake to late apex
- Rotate car early so you can unwind steering
- Smooth, progressive throttle (wheelspin = death)

**Examples:**
- Jefferson T3 (28-31 mph)
- Winton T2, T7 (34-37 mph)
- Rudskogen T5 (35-40 mph)

**Ray FF1600 Tip:** The open diff HATES harsh throttle out of hairpins. Wheelspin = understeer = slow exit.

---

### 2. **Carousel**

**What it is:**
- Long, constant-radius corner (90-180+ degrees)
- Medium speed, extended duration (3-5+ seconds)
- Usually 3rd gear in Ray FF1600
- Entry: ~45-65 mph

**Characteristics:**
- You're turning for a LONG time
- Constant/consistent radius (doesn't tighten or open much)
- Requires patience and consistent steering angle
- Can't rush it - have to "ride" it around

**Technique:**
- Early brake (earlier than you think!)
- Long, feathered trail brake deep into corner
- Late apex approach
- Maintain consistent steering angle (don't saw!)
- Feed throttle in gradually as you open steering

**Examples:**
- Jefferson T7 "Long Right Carousel" (47-53 mph)
- Rudskogen T11-T12 "Carousel Right" (55-62 mph)
- Winton T10 "Long Right Sweeper" (59-65 mph, carousel-like)

**Ray FF1600 Tip:** Patience wins. Adding more steering mid-carousel = understeer. Trust the initial rotation.

**Why called "Carousel":** Like a fairground merry-go-round, you're going in a circle for an extended time!

---

### 3. **Sweeper**

**What it is:**
- Fast, flowing corner with gentle radius
- High speed, minimal steering input
- Usually 4th gear (or high 3rd) in Ray FF1600
- Entry: ~70-95+ mph

**Characteristics:**
- Large radius, gentle arc
- Brief duration (1-2 seconds)
- Minimal braking (lift or light brush)
- Focus on smooth inputs and carrying speed

**Technique:**
- Small lift or light brake to settle car
- Minimal, smooth steering input
- Keep throttle as constant as possible
- Don't over-slow!

**Examples:**
- Jefferson T2 "Left Kink" (70-78 mph)
- Rudskogen T2 "Left Kink" (85-95 mph)
- Winton T1 "Right Sweeper" (65-71 mph)

**Ray FF1600 Tip:** The car has no aero, so it won't magically stick. Smooth hands = fast exit.

---

### 4. **90-Degree Turn** (Standard Corner)

**What it is:**
- Medium-radius, 90-degree corner
- Moderate speed reduction required
- Usually 2nd or 3rd gear
- Entry: ~35-60 mph

**Characteristics:**
- Classic "brake, turn, apex, exit" corner
- Not as tight as hairpin, not as long as carousel
- Quick rotation needed but not extreme

**Technique:**
- Standard trail braking approach
- Late apex preferred (unless setting up next corner)
- Progressive throttle on exit
- Focus on exit speed

**Examples:**
- Jefferson T1 "Right 90¬∞" (37-40 mph)
- Rudskogen T1 (60-65 mph)
- Most "standard" corners on any track

**Ray FF1600 Tip:** Don't over-brake. The car carries momentum better than you think.

---

### 5. **Kink** (Fast Direction Change)

**What it is:**
- Very fast, slight direction change
- Minimal or no braking
- Usually flat-out or tiny lift
- Entry: ~80-110 mph

**Characteristics:**
- Small steering angle (< 20-30 degrees)
- Brief (under 1 second)
- More about car balance than technique
- Often on straights or between corners

**Technique:**
- Tiny lift (if needed) to settle car
- Minimal, precise steering
- Back to full throttle ASAP
- Keep hands calm

**Examples:**
- Jefferson T2 "Left Kink" (no brake, small lift)
- Jefferson T4 "Left Kink on Back Straight" (flat)
- Rudskogen T6 "Fast Right Kink" (flat)

**Ray FF1600 Tip:** If you're braking hard in a "kink," you're doing it wrong!

## üîó Corner Combinations

### 1. **Chicane**

**What it is:**
- Quick left-right (or right-left) combination
- Designed to slow cars on straights
- Usually linked with minimal straight between

**Characteristics:**
- First corner sets up second corner
- Often compromises both apexes
- Focus on exit of final corner

**Technique:**
- Brake once for the whole sequence
- Compromise first apex to set up second
- Roll speed through, don't stop and start
- Exit speed from last corner is priority

**Examples:**
- Not prominent on Jefferson/Rudskogen/Winton
- Common on F1 tracks (Monza chicanes, etc.)

**Ray FF1600 Tip:** Maintain flow. Two separate brake zones = you're over-slowing.

---

### 2. **Esses** (S-Curves)

**What it is:**
- Series of linked corners alternating direction
- Usually medium-to-fast speed
- Flow and rhythm critical

**Characteristics:**
- Each corner affects the next
- Compromise lines to maintain flow
- Consistent speed more important than peak speed
- Often 3-5 corners in sequence

**Technique:**
- Think of it as ONE long corner, not separate corners
- Light, brief braking inputs
- Rolling speed through > peak speed in one corner
- Keep steering smooth and progressive
- Exit of LAST corner is what matters

**Examples:**
- **Jefferson T5-T6 "Esses"** (Right then Left, 53-65 mph)
- **Rudskogen T3-T4 "Downhill Esses"** (Right then Left)
- **Winton T8-T9 "Esses"** (Left then Right, 65-75 mph)

**Ray FF1600 Tip:** Over-drive the first corner = struggle through rest of sequence. Patience!

---

### 3. **Switchback**

**What it is:**
- Tight corners in opposite directions
- Close together but distinct
- Often uphill/downhill

**Characteristics:**
- First corner is often slower
- Quick transition between them
- Position for second corner is critical

**Technique:**
- Sacrifice first corner to set up second
- Quick steering reversal
- Focus on exit of final corner

**Examples:**
- Winton T4 "Left Switchback" after T3
- Rudskogen has several in technical sections

---

### 4. **Double Apex**

**What it is:**
- Long corner with two distinct apex points
- One corner, but requires two inputs
- Often goes around obstacles or elevation

**Characteristics:**
- First apex is compromise
- Second apex is the "real" apex
- Long duration like carousel but with two peaks

**Technique:**
- Turn in for first apex
- Maintenance throttle between apexes
- Treat second apex as exit point
- Early throttle after second apex

**Examples:**
- Rudskogen T2 (Master Lonn's description: "3rd on first apex, 2nd before hill to second apex")
- Some carousel corners have double-apex characteristic

**Ray FF1600 Tip:** Don't rush the transition between apexes. Let the car settle.

## üèÅ Track Parts & Features

### **Apex**

**What it is:** The innermost point of your line through a corner (closest to inside edge).

**Types:**
- **Early Apex:** Turn in early, apex early ‚Üí BAD (run wide on exit, no throttle room)
- **Late Apex:** Turn in later, apex 2/3+ into corner ‚Üí GOOD (opens up exit, earlier throttle)
- **Diamond Apex:** Ultra-late apex, almost at exit ‚Üí BEST for hairpins (maximum exit speed)

**Ray FF1600 Focus:** Late apex almost always wins. Exit speed > entry speed.

---

### **Racing Line**

**What it is:** The path through a corner that minimizes time (usually).

**Classic Racing Line:**
1. Enter wide
2. Turn in at specific point
3. Clip late apex
4. Exit wide (use all track)

**When to compromise:** Next corner is more important (like in esses).

---

### **Track Limits**

**What it is:** White lines at edge of track. Going beyond = penalty (off-track, 1x).

**Rules:**
- At least 2 wheels inside white line at all times
- Going beyond repeatedly = time penalty
- Curbs are usually "track" but check rules

---

### **Curbs (Kerbs)**

**What it is:** Raised, painted edges of corners.

**Types:**
- **Flat/Ripple Curbs:** Safe to use, can help rotation (entry) or open up corner (exit)
- **Sausage Curbs:** Tall, chunky curbs ‚Üí AVOID in Ray FF1600! Will unsettle car badly.

**Ray FF1600 Tip:** Gentle brushing of flat curbs = good. Aggressive sausage curb = spin/damage.

---

### **Camber**

**What it is:** Banking or slope of the track surface.

**Types:**
- **Positive Camber:** Track slopes toward inside of corner ‚Üí Helps grip!
- **Negative/Off-Camber:** Track slopes away from inside ‚Üí Reduces grip, scary
- **Flat:** No camber

**Ray FF1600 Tip:** Positive camber = be brave. Off-camber = be gentle.

---

### **Elevation Change**

**What it is:** Uphill/downhill sections of track.

**Effects on Driving:**
- **Downhill Braking:** Car gets light, less braking grip (brake earlier/softer)
- **Uphill Braking:** Car compressed, MORE braking grip (can brake later/harder)
- **Crest (top of hill):** Car goes light, may lose grip
- **Compression (bottom of valley):** Car loads up, more grip available

**Examples:**
- **Rudskogen T2:** Downhill braking (tricky! brake at "50 sign" before crest)
- **Jefferson T3:** Slight downhill into hairpin

---

### **Braking Zones**

**What it is:** The section of track where you slow the car.

**Key Points:**
- **Brake Marker:** Reference point to start braking (100m board, sign, cone, etc.)
- **Straight-line Braking:** Initial hard brake before turning
- **Trail Braking:** Gradually release brake as you turn in (keeps weight on front)

**Ray FF1600 Tip:** Long, smooth trail brake = rotation. Stabbing brake = spin.

---

### **Overtaking Zones**

**Common overtaking corners:**
1. **End of longest straight** (into heavy braking zone)
2. **After key exit corner** (draft + better exit = pass next corner)
3. **Hairpins** (out-brake opponent, inside line)

**Defensive lines:**
- Cover inside early
- Brake slightly earlier
- Focus on exit, not apex defense

---

## üìä Corner Priority System

Not all corners are equal! Focus on corners that matter:

### **Critical Corners** (Gain 0.2-0.5s per lap!)
1. **Last corner before longest straight** ‚Üí Exit speed = lap time
2. **Hairpins onto straights** ‚Üí Same reason
3. **Corner that sets up a sequence** ‚Üí Get it wrong, whole sequence suffers

### **Medium Priority**
- Middle of lap corners
- Technical sequences (get them right for flow)

### **Low Priority**
- Corners that lead into more corners (compromise for next corner)
- Fast kinks (hard to gain time, easy to lose it)

**Focus 80% of practice on the critical corners!**

## üèéÔ∏è Ray FF1600-Specific Corner Advice

### Low-Speed (Hairpins, tight 90s)
- **Challenge:** Open diff = wheelspin = understeer
- **Solution:** Smooth, progressive throttle application
- **Focus:** Rotate early, unwind steering, THEN throttle

### Medium-Speed (Carousels, standard corners)
- **Challenge:** No aero = car won't magically stick
- **Solution:** Trail braking for rotation, patient throttle
- **Focus:** Late apex, let car settle before full throttle

### High-Speed (Sweepers, kinks)
- **Challenge:** Car is light, minimal aero
- **Solution:** Smooth hands, small inputs, trust the grip
- **Focus:** Carry momentum, don't over-brake
