**Technique:**

1. **Brake hard** (straight line)
2. **Trail brake** to late apex
3. **Rotate early** so you can unwind steering
4. **Smooth throttle** (wheelspin kills exit speed)

**Why It Works:**

Hairpins are all about **exit speed**. You're going slow anyway, so entry speed doesn't matter. What matters is how early you can get on throttle and how hard you can accelerate out.

**Late apex** = earlier throttle = faster exit = faster down the next straight.

**Examples:**

- **Jefferson T3:** 28-31 mph hairpin
- **Winton T2, T7:** 34-37 mph hairpins
- **Rudskogen T5:** 35-40 mph hairpin

**Why Called "Carousel":**

**Technique:**

1. **Early brake** (earlier than you think!)
2. **Long trail brake** deep into corner
3. **Late apex** approach
4. **Consistent steering angle** (don't saw!)
5. **Patient throttle** as you unwind

**Why It's Tricky:**

Carousels test **patience**. The corner lasts so long that you're tempted to add more steering mid-corner (because it feels like you're not turning enough). But adding steering = loading outside tires more = understeer.

**The secret:** Trust your initial rotation. Let the car settle into the arc.

**Examples:**

- **Jefferson T7:** Long Right Carousel (47-53 mph)
- **Rudskogen T11-T12:** Carousel Right (55-62 mph)
- **Winton T10:** Long Right Sweeper (carousel-like, 59-65 mph)

**Ray FF1600 Tip:**

Patience wins. Adding more steering mid-carousel = understeer. Trust the initial rotation.

**Technique:**

1. **Small lift** or light brake (settle car)
2. **Minimal steering** input
3. **Constant throttle** as much as possible
4. **Don't over-slow!** (common mistake)

**Why It Works:**

Sweepers are about **momentum**. You're carrying high speed through a gentle arc. The less you disturb the car (sudden inputs), the faster you'll be.

**Common mistake:** Braking too much. The corner is faster than you think!

**Examples:**

- **Jefferson T2:** Left Kink/Sweeper (70-78 mph)
- **Rudskogen T2:** Left Kink (85-95 mph)
- **Winton T1:** Right Sweeper (65-71 mph)

**Ray FF1600 Tip:**

The car has NO aero, so it won't magically stick. Smooth hands = fast exit.

**Master Lonn's Application:**

You've learned that sweepers feel faster than they are. Trust the car's grip and commit!

## 5. Kink (Fast Direction Change) âš¡

**Definition:**
- Very fast, slight direction change
- Minimal or no braking
- Usually flat-out or tiny lift
- Entry: ~80-110 mph

**Visual:**

**Technique:**

1. **Tiny lift** (if needed) to settle car
2. **Minimal, precise steering**
3. **Back to full throttle ASAP**
4. **Keep hands calm**

**Why It's Different:**

Kinks aren't really "corners" in the traditional sense. They're high-speed direction changes where you're trying to disturb the car as little as possible.

**The key:** Smoothness. Jerky steering at 90+ mph = big problem.

**Examples:**

- **Jefferson T2:** Left Kink (no brake, small lift)
- **Jefferson T4:** Left Kink on Back Straight (flat)
- **Rudskogen T6:** Fast Right Kink (flat)

**Ray FF1600 Tip:**

If you're braking HARD in a "kink," you're doing it wrong! It should be flat-out or a tiny lift at most.

**Master Lonn's Realization:**

Kinks feel scarier than they are. The FF1600 has enough grip to take them flat if you're smooth.
