## Curbs (Kerbs) ğŸŸ¥â¬œğŸŸ¥

**Definition:** Raised, painted edges of corners (usually red/white striped).

### Types

**1. Flat/Ripple Curbs** âœ…

**What they are:**
- Low-profile, rippled surface
- Usually red/white striped

**Safe to use:**
- Entry curbs: Can help rotation (brief weight transfer)
- Exit curbs: Can open up the corner (use more track)
- Apex curbs: Can shorten the corner slightly

**Technique:**
- **Brush** the curb (light touch)
- Don't **ride** the curb (prolonged contact)

---

**2. Sausage Curbs** âŒ

**What they are:**
- Tall, chunky, cylindrical curbs
- Often bright colored
- Designed to DETER cutting

**AVOID in Ray FF1600!**
- Will unsettle the car badly
- Can cause loss of control
- Can damage suspension

**Why they exist:** To prevent corner cutting (track limits enforcement).

**Rule:** If you see sausage curbs, stay away!

---

**Ray FF1600 Curb Strategy:**

- Gentle brushing of flat curbs = good
- Aggressive sausage curb = spin/damage
- When in doubt, stay on tarmac

## Camber ğŸ“

**Definition:** Banking or slope of the track surface.

### Types

**Positive Camber (Banked)** âœ…

**What it is:**
- Track slopes TOWARD the inside of the corner
- Like a NASCAR oval

**Effect:**
- **Helps grip!** (gravity works with you)
- Car feels more stable
- Can carry more speed

**Approach:**
- Be brave
- Carry more speed than flat corner
- Trust the banking

---

**Negative/Off-Camber** âš ï¸

**What it is:**
- Track slopes AWAY from the inside
- Gravity pulls you toward the outside

**Effect:**
- **Reduces grip** (gravity works against you)
- Car feels unstable
- Requires caution

**Approach:**
- Be gentle
- Less speed than you'd expect
- Smooth inputs critical

---

**Flat Camber**

**What it is:**
- No slope, level track

**Effect:**
- Standard grip
- Predictable behavior

---

**Ray FF1600 Tip:**

Positive camber = be brave. Off-camber = be gentle.

**Master Lonn's Experience:**

You've felt the differenceâ€”banked corners feel more secure, off-camber feels sketchy. That's camber at work!

## Elevation Change â›°ï¸

**Definition:** Uphill/downhill sections of track.

### Effects on Driving

**Downhill Braking** â¬‡ï¸

**What happens:**
- Car gets light (weight shifts forward)
- Less braking grip available
- Rear wheels unload (can lock easily)

**Solution:**
- Brake **earlier**
- Brake **softer**
- Be gentle on pedal inputs

---

**Uphill Braking** â¬†ï¸

**What happens:**
- Car compressed (weight shifts rearward)
- MORE braking grip available
- Front wheels plant

**Solution:**
- Can brake **later**
- Can brake **harder**
- Take advantage of extra grip

---

**Crest (Top of Hill)** ğŸ”

**What happens:**
- Car goes light (vertical load reduces)
- May lose grip momentarily
- Suspension unloads

**Danger:**
- If turning while cresting = possible loss of grip
- If braking while cresting = possible lockup

**Solution:**
- Finish braking before crest
- Gentle inputs over the top
- Anticipate weight shift

---

**Compression (Bottom of Valley)** â¬‡ï¸â¬†ï¸

**What happens:**
- Car loads up (vertical load increases)
- More grip available
- Suspension compresses

**Opportunity:**
- Can carry more speed
- Can brake later
- Can turn harder

**Solution:**
- Use the extra grip
- Be aggressive (but smooth)

---

**Examples:**

- **Rudskogen T2:** Downhill braking (tricky! Brake at "50 sign" before crest)
- **Jefferson T3:** Slight downhill into hairpin
