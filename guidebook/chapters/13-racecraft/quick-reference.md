## Core Principle

Racing isn't about being fastest in isolation. It's about being **fastest relative to competitors** while managing **risk**. Finish the race before you try to win it.

---

## Golden Rules

- **Make the race, don't break it** - 80% of results = staying out of trouble
- **Predictability = Safety** - Hold your line, brake consistently, telegraph moves
- **One defensive move per straight** - Commit early, don't weave
- **Overtake in zones, not dive-bombs** - Plan the pass, execute cleanly

---

## Situational Awareness

**Check mirrors:**

- Every brake zone
- Before defensive moves
- After completing passes

**Awareness zones:**

- **Zone 1 (2 car lengths):** Immediate threat - most attention
- **Zone 2 (5 car lengths):** Planning phase - some attention
- **Zone 3 (10+ lengths):** No immediate concern - minimal attention

---

## Incident Management

**If you're hit:**

1. Regain control
2. Execute 10-Second Rule (Chapter 12)
3. Don't retaliate
4. Adjust goals if needed

**If you cause incident:**

1. Slow down, give space
2. Return position if you gained advantage
3. Apologize after race
