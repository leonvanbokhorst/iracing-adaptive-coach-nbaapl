## The Lesson: "Meebewegen"

**Dutch word, no direct English translation. Closest: "Moving with" or "Going with the flow."**

When I'm racing scared, I'm **fighting the car**:

- Tight hands
- Tense shoulders
- Jerky inputs
- Overdriving

When I'm racing calm, I'm **dancing with the car**:

- Smooth inputs
- Trust in technique
- Letting it flow
- Meebewegen

**The problem:**

- In practice, I can practice meebewegen consciously
- In racing, when the pressure hits, I revert to **survival mode**
- Survival mode â‰  smooth
- Survival mode = fighting

**The fix:**
I need to practice meebewegen **under pressure**, so it becomes automatic.

Because when the race is on, my thinking brain shuts down. The **practiced brain** takes over.

And right now, my practiced brain under pressure is: "GRIP HARDER, FIGHT MORE, DON'T LOSE."

That needs to change.

## What Needs to Happen: The Practice Plan

### 1. **Reframe the Fear**

**Current mindset:**

> "Fear of being overtaken"

**New mindset:**

> "Curiosity about what they'll try"

When someone is behind me, they're not a threat. They're a **teacher**:

- Where are they faster?
- What line are they taking?
- Can I learn from them?

Make it a **game**, not a **threat**.

---

### 2. **Practice Pressure Situations: The "Let Them By" Drill**

**Next practice session at ANY track:**

1. Drive 5 laps at race pace
2. When someone catches me, **let them pass**
3. Follow them for 2-3 laps
4. Try to learn their line
5. **Don't try to re-pass**

**Goal:** Desensitize myself to being overtaken. Practice staying calm when someone is faster.

**Why it works:**
Being overtaken won't feel like "losing" anymore. It'll feel like "data collection."

---

### 3. **The "S3 Left-Hander" Ritual**

I spun twice in the **same corner**.

**Before the corner:**

- Breathe out (release tension)
- Say (in my head): "Smooth, not fast"
- Focus on **exit**, not entry

**In the corner:**

- Feel the weight transfer (meebewegen)
- Trust the car
- If it slides, **let it slide** (don't fight it)

**After the corner:**

- "Good" or "Learn" (no judgment)

---

### 4. **The Contract Reminder Card**

Literally write this on a sticky note and put it on my wheel base:

```
FOCUS: LEARNING
NOT: WINNING

FLOW > FEAR
```

Read it before EVERY race. Out loud. Once.
